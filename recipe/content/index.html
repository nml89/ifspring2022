Honey Garlic Glazed Salmon

Description:

A delicious seafood meal that you're sure to never forget! The honey, soy sauce, and garlic perfectly compliment the salmon without taking over its natural flavor. This quick and easy meal takes about 20 minutes to make with 5 minutes of prep time. It is best served with a white rice of your choice. A large skillet of any kind is the perfect pan for this dish.

Remember to check for bones in your salmon before cooking! To do this: cut your filets and run your fingers along the surface. If you feel any pricks or resistance, remove the bone using kitchen tweezers or your fingers.

This dish contains soy and fish.

Recipe Source Peer Review

Ingredients:

(Serves four)

  1. 1/3 cup honey
  2. 1/4 cup low-sodium soy sauce
  3. 2 tbsp lemon juice
  4. 1 tsp. red pepper flakes
  5. 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  6. 4 6-oz salmon filets, patted dry
  7. Kosher salt
  8. Black pepper
  9. 3 cloves garlic, minced
  10. 1 lemon, sliced into rounds

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together the honey, soy sauce, lemon juice and red pepper flakes.
  2. In a large skillet over medium-high heat, heat two tablespoons of oil. When the oil is hot, but not smoking, add the salmon skin-side up and season with salt and pepper. Cook the salmon until it's deeply golden (about 6 minutes), flip over and add the remaining tablespoon of oil.
  3. Add garlic to the skillet and cook until fragrant (1 minute). Add the honey mixture and sliced lemons and cook until the sauce is reduced by about 1/3. Baste salmon with the sauce.
  4. Garnish with sliced lemon and serve hot.
  5. Refrigerate leftovers in a tight container for up to three days.
